## 1. Minimal Background Notation

> [!notation] Notation 1.1: Ambient conventions
> Work in $\mathsf{ZFC}$. For a set $S$, $S^{<\omega}=\bigcup_{n\in\mathbb N}S^n$; $\varepsilon$ is the empty word; $\coprod$ is tagged disjoint union; $\prod$ is the product/dependent product. Composition $g\circ f$ applies $f$ first. Definitional equality is $:=$.

> [!notation] Notation 1.2: Indexed families
> An $I$-indexed family of sets is $(A_i)_{i\in I}$. An indexed family of maps $(h_i:A_i\to B_i)_i$ acts componentwise.

> [!principle] Principle 1.3: Translation rule
> Every ordinary construction is performed **sortwise**:
> $$
> |A| \rightsquigarrow (|A|_s)_{s\in S},
> \qquad
> h \rightsquigarrow (h_s)_{s\in S},
> \qquad
> \theta \rightsquigarrow (\theta_s)_{s\in S}.
> $$

## 2. Single-Sorted Baseline, Compressed

> [!definition] Definition 2.1: Signature/algebra/homomorphism
> A signature $\Omega=(\Sigma,\operatorname{ar})$ has operation symbols $f\in\Sigma$ with arity $\operatorname{ar}(f)=n$. An $\Omega$-algebra $A$ has carrier $|A|$ and operations $f^A:|A|^n\to |A|$. A homomorphism $h:A\to B$ satisfies
> $$
> h(f^A(\bar a))=f^B(h\bar a).
> $$

> [!definition] Definition 2.2: Term algebra/free algebra
> $T_\Omega(X)$ is generated by variables $X$, constants, and formal applications $f(t_1,\dots,t_n)$. The term algebra $\mathbf T_\Omega(X)$ has formal operations. It is **free on $X$** iff every assignment $g:X\to |A|$ extends uniquely to a homomorphism
> $$
> \operatorname{ev}_g:\mathbf T_\Omega(X)\to A.
> $$

> [!definition] Definition 2.3: Congruence/quotient/presentation
> A congruence $\theta$ on $A$ is an operation-compatible equivalence relation. $A/\theta$ has carrier $|A|/\theta$ and operations on classes. For equations $E\subseteq T_\Omega(X)^2$,
> $$
> \langle X\mid E\rangle := \mathbf T_\Omega(X)/\operatorname{Cg}(E).
> $$

> [!definition] Definition 2.4: Substitution
> A substitution $\sigma:X\to T_\Omega(Y)$ induces $\widehat\sigma:\mathbf T_\Omega(X)\to\mathbf T_\Omega(Y)$; write $t\sigma$. Composition is Kleisli composition:
> $$
> (\sigma;\tau)(x)=(\sigma(x))\tau,
> \qquad
> t(\sigma;\tau)=(t\sigma)\tau.
> $$

# Part I вЂ” Many-Sorted / Typed Universal Algebra

## 3. Sorted Signatures

> [!definition] Definition 3.1: Many-sorted signature
> Fix a set $S$ of **sorts**. An $S$-sorted signature is
> $$
> \Sigma=(\mathrm{Op},\operatorname{type}),
> \qquad
> \operatorname{type}:\mathrm{Op}\to S^{<\omega}\times S.
> $$
> If $\operatorname{type}(f)=(s_1\cdots s_n,s)$, write
> $$
> f:s_1\cdots s_n\to s
> \quad\text{or}\quad
> f:w\to s.
> $$
> $w=s_1\cdots s_n$ is the input profile; $s$ is the result sort. Constants have $w=\varepsilon$, written $f:{\to}s$.

> [!notation] Notation 3.2: Arity/profile
> For $w=s_1\cdots s_n\in S^{<\omega}$, $|w|=n$. A signature is finitary when all profiles are finite. If $S=\{*\}$, the many-sorted definitions reduce to the ordinary single-sorted ones.

## 4. Sorted Algebras

> [!definition] Definition 4.1: $\Sigma$-algebra
> A many-sorted $\Sigma$-algebra $A$ consists of carriers $(|A|_s)_{s\in S}$ and, for each $f:s_1\cdots s_n\to s$,
> $$
> f^A:|A|_{s_1}\times\cdots\times |A|_{s_n}\to |A|_s.
> $$
> A constant $f:{\to}s$ is an element $f^A\in |A|_s$.

> [!definition] Definition 4.2: Sorted homomorphism
> A homomorphism $h:A\to B$ is a family $(h_s:|A|_s\to |B|_s)_{s\in S}$ such that, for every $f:s_1\cdots s_n\to s$,
> $$
> h_s(f^A(a_1,\dots,a_n))
> =
> f^B(h_{s_1}(a_1),\dots,h_{s_n}(a_n)).
> $$
> Embedding $\iff$ every $h_s$ injective. Isomorphism $\iff$ every $h_s$ bijective and homomorphic inverse exists. Category: $\mathbf{Alg}(\Sigma)$.

> [!definition] Definition 4.3: Subalgebras/products/generated subalgebra
> A sorted subset $C=(C_s)_s$ of $A$ is closed iff
> $$
> a_i\in C_{s_i}\ (i=1,\dots,n),\ f:s_1\cdots s_n\to s
> \Longrightarrow
> f^A(\bar a)\in C_s.
> $$
> Closed sorted subsets are subalgebras $C\le A$. Products are sortwise:
> $$
> \left|\prod_i A_i\right|_s=\prod_i |A_i|_s.
> $$
> $\langle G\rangle_A$ is the least closed sorted superset of $G=(G_s)_s$.

## 5. Sorted Terms and Free Algebras

> [!definition] Definition 5.1: Sorted variables/terms
> A sorted variable family is $X=(X_s)_{s\in S}$, pairwise disjoint and disjoint from $\mathrm{Op}$. The term family $T_\Sigma(X)=(T_\Sigma(X)_s)_s$ is generated by
> $$
> \frac{x\in X_s}{x\in T_\Sigma(X)_s}
> \qquad
> \frac{t_i\in T_\Sigma(X)_{s_i}\ (i=1,\dots,n)}{f(t_1,\dots,t_n)\in T_\Sigma(X)_s}
> \quad(f:s_1\cdots s_n\to s).
> $$
> Each term has a unique sort.

> [!construction] Construction 5.2: Sorted term algebra
> $\mathbf T_\Sigma(X)$ has carrier $(T_\Sigma(X)_s)_s$, formal operations
> $$
> f^{\mathbf T}(t_1,\dots,t_n):=f(t_1,\dots,t_n),
> $$
> and insertion $\eta_X=(\eta_{X,s})_s$, $\eta_{X,s}(x)=x$.

> [!theorem] Theorem 5.3: Sorted universal mapping property
> $\mathbf T_\Sigma(X)$ is free on $X$: for every $A\in\mathbf{Alg}(\Sigma)$ and sorted assignment $g=(g_s:X_s\to |A|_s)_s$, there is a unique homomorphism
> $$
> \operatorname{ev}_g:\mathbf T_\Sigma(X)\to A
> $$
> with $\operatorname{ev}_g\circ\eta_X=g$. Recursion:
> $$
> \operatorname{ev}_g(x)=g_s(x),
> \qquad
> \operatorname{ev}_g(f(\bar t))=f^A(\operatorname{ev}_g(\bar t)).
> $$
> Hence
> $$
> \mathbf T_\Sigma(-):\mathbf{Set}^S \rightleftarrows \mathbf{Alg}(\Sigma):U
> $$
> is a freeвЂ“forgetful adjunction, with $\mathbf T_\Sigma\dashv U$.

> [!definition] Definition 5.4: Sorted polynomial functor
> The signature endofunctor $H_\Sigma:\mathbf{Set}^S\to\mathbf{Set}^S$ is
> $$
> (H_\Sigma Y)_s
> :=
> \coprod_{f:s_1\cdots s_n\to s}
> Y_{s_1}\times\cdots\times Y_{s_n}.
> $$
> $H_\Sigma$-algebras are $\Sigma$-algebras. Ground terms $\mathbf T_\Sigma(\varnothing)$ form the initial $H_\Sigma$-algebra. $\mathbf T_\Sigma(X)$ is the free $H_\Sigma$-algebra on $X$.

> [!warning] Warning 5.5: Empty sorts
> If $X_s=\varnothing$ and no ground term of sort $s$ exists, then $T_\Sigma(X)_s=\varnothing$. Empty term-sorts and empty carriers are allowed. They matter for equational logic (В§8).

## 6. Sorted Congruences and Quotients

> [!definition] Definition 6.1: Sorted congruence/quotient
> A sorted congruence on $A$ is $\theta=(\theta_s)_{s\in S}$, each $\theta_s$ an equivalence relation on $|A|_s$, such that
> $$
> (a_i,b_i)\in\theta_{s_i}\ \forall i
> \Longrightarrow
> (f^A(\bar a),f^A(\bar b))\in\theta_s
> $$
> for every $f:s_1\cdots s_n\to s$. The quotient has carriers
> $$
> |A/\theta|_s:=|A|_s/\theta_s,
> $$
> with operations on classes. $\operatorname{Con}(A)$ is a complete lattice under sortwise inclusion.

> [!theorem] Theorem 6.2: Sorted isomorphism theorems
> For $h:A\to B$,
> $$
> (\ker h)_s:=\{(a,a')\in |A|_s^2:h_s(a)=h_s(a')\}
> $$
> is a sorted congruence, $\operatorname{im}(h)\le B$, and
> $$
> A/\ker h\cong\operatorname{im}(h),
> \qquad
> [a]\mapsto h_s(a).
> $$
> Correspondence and second/third isomorphism theorems hold sortwise. Every $\Sigma$-algebra is a quotient of a sorted term algebra.

## 7. Sorted Substitution and the Term Monad

> [!definition] Definition 7.1: Sorted substitution
> A sorted substitution $\sigma:X\to T_\Sigma(Y)$ is a family
> $$
> \sigma_s:X_s\to T_\Sigma(Y)_s.
> $$
> Its extension $\widehat\sigma:\mathbf T_\Sigma(X)\to\mathbf T_\Sigma(Y)$ is evaluation into syntax. Write $t\sigma$.

> [!theorem] Theorem 7.2: Substitution discipline
> Substitution preserves sorts:
> $$
> t\in T_\Sigma(X)_s \Longrightarrow t\sigma\in T_\Sigma(Y)_s.
> $$
> Composition is Kleisli composition:
> $$
> (\sigma;\tau)(x):=(\sigma(x))\tau,
> \qquad
> t(\sigma;\tau)=(t\sigma)\tau.
> $$
> Thus $(T_\Sigma,\eta,\mu)$ is a monad on $\mathbf{Set}^S$, and
> $$
> \mathbf{Alg}(\Sigma)\cong (\mathbf{Set}^S)^{T_\Sigma}
> $$
> as the EilenbergвЂ“Moore category.

## 8. Sorted Equational Logic

> [!definition] Definition 8.1: Sorted equation/satisfaction
> A sorted equation is context-carrying data
> $$
> (\Gamma,t,u),
> \qquad
> t,u\in T_\Sigma(\Gamma)_\sigma,
> $$
> written $\Gamma\vdash t\approx u$. A $\Sigma$-algebra $A$ satisfies it iff
> $$
> \operatorname{ev}_v(t)=\operatorname{ev}_v(u)
> $$
> for every sorted assignment $v:\Gamma\to |A|$.

> [!theorem] Theorem 8.2: Sound/complete sorted equational calculus
> With explicit contexts, the usual rulesвЂ”reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, congruence, substitutionвЂ”are sound and complete:
> $$
> E\vdash(\Gamma,t,u)
> \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
> E\models(\Gamma,t,u).
> $$

> [!construction] Construction 8.3: Presented/free algebra of a variety
> Let $\theta_E^{\mathrm{fi}}$ be the least fully invariant sorted congruence containing $E$. Then the free algebra in the variety defined by $E$ on $X$ is
> $$
> \mathbf T_\Sigma(X)/\theta_E^{\mathrm{fi}}.
> $$

> [!warning] Warning 8.4: Empty-carrier anomaly
> If contexts are omitted, many-sorted equational logic can become unsound when some sorts are empty. Repairs:
> $$
> \text{explicit contexts}
> \quad\text{or}\quad
> \text{all sorts inhabited}
> \quad\text{or}\quad
> \text{Goguen--Meseguer variant calculus.}
> $$

> [!theorem] Theorem 8.5: Many-sorted Birkhoff theorem
> A class of $\Sigma$-algebras is a variety iff it is closed under sorted homomorphic images, subalgebras, and products, iff it is axiomatizable by context-carrying sorted equations.
